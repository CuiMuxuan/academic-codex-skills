# Section Playbooks

Use this reference when the user needs section-specific polishing rather than only sentence-level cleanup. Each playbook explains what usually makes that section look AI-shaped, what must be preserved, and what kind of revision tends to produce a more native and authorial academic voice.

## How To Use

1. Identify the manuscript section.
2. Diagnose the most obvious section-level risk.
3. Apply the relevant playbook below before doing sentence-level edits.
4. Use `high-risk-patterns.md` only after the section-level structure is under control.

## Abstract

### Common AI-shaped signals

- every sentence is evenly polished and similarly long
- novelty, scope, method, and implication are distributed with suspicious neatness
- the abstract sounds like a "review summary template" rather than a paper-specific claim
- phrases such as "systematically summarizes", "provides important guidance", and "offers valuable insights" dominate

### What must be preserved

- the actual contribution and scope
- the organizing claim of the manuscript
- any real methodological boundary

### Best revision moves

- replace broad promotional wording with a sharper review angle
- make one sentence more concrete than elegant
- avoid ending with a ceremonial "future guidance" line unless it says something specific
- let novelty emerge from the problem framing, not from adjectives

### Final check

- does the abstract sound conditional and field-aware rather than universally polished?
- is there at least one sentence that names the actual analytical lens of the paper?

## Highlights

### Common AI-shaped signals

- all bullets have the same grammatical pattern
- bullets sound like slogans
- novelty is overstated through generic academic adjectives

### What must be preserved

- the real takeaways
- journal length limits

### Best revision moves

- vary bullet syntax slightly
- prefer technical consequences over abstract praise
- remove inflated words such as `comprehensive`, `systematic`, or `important` unless they are essential

## Introduction

### Common AI-shaped signals

- polished critique delivered as perfect oppositions
- repeated "existing studies/reviews are useful, however..." turns
- very smooth confidence with little local asymmetry
- novelty claim sounds staged rather than necessary

### What must be preserved

- the paper's motivation
- the problem scope
- the logic that justifies the paper's structure or novelty

### Best revision moves

- anchor the introduction in a real decision problem, not just a topic importance claim
- reduce symmetrical critique
- let one sentence expose what the field gets wrong or leaves unresolved
- make the novelty claim narrower and more defensible

### Final check

- does the introduction sound like it was written by someone with a stake in the problem?
- does the novelty arise from a practical gap rather than a generic literature gap?

## Literature Review / Technology Sections

### Common AI-shaped signals

- every subsection follows the same "mechanism -> advantages -> limitations -> prospects" skeleton
- repeated "X is effective, but Y remains challenging" sentences
- performance claims are not tied to mechanism or operating boundary
- summary dominates over judgment

### What must be preserved

- the evidence base
- process terminology
- citation logic

### Best revision moves

- organize one section around mechanism, another around failure route, another around integration role
- convert descriptive balance into engineering evaluation
- qualify headline removal data with runtime, loading, humidity, selectivity, or by-product burden
- include one selective judgment sentence per subsection where justified

### Final check

- can the reader tell what should not be overinterpreted?
- is each subsection doing more than repeating the same review rhythm?

## Results Discussion or Critical Synthesis

### Common AI-shaped signals

- everything is summarized at the same altitude
- synthesis sentences are polished but empty
- metrics are treated as self-explanatory

### What must be preserved

- the evidentiary logic
- distinctions between stronger and weaker support

### Best revision moves

- state what the evidence supports and what it does not
- turn summary into selection
- add operating or methodological qualifiers to metric-based claims

## Hybrid Design / Process Selection Sections

### Common AI-shaped signals

- route I / route II / route III prose uses the same sentence frame
- list structure overpowers argument
- route descriptions sound taxonomic rather than analytical

### What must be preserved

- the decision logic
- the role of each sub-unit
- any techno-economic or control burden discussion

### Best revision moves

- frame route comparison as burden allocation rather than equipment stacking
- vary the prose inside each route
- emphasize the specific failure the route is designed to prevent
- avoid checklist wording where trade-off logic is possible

### Final check

- does each route sound justified by a different engineering need?
- would a reader understand why one route fails where another remains credible?

## Future Perspectives / Research Gaps

### Common AI-shaped signals

- "more studies are needed" boilerplate
- generic calls for standardization
- future work reads like a universal wish list

### What must be preserved

- real unresolved problems
- boundaries between scientific, reporting, and engineering gaps

### Best revision moves

- specify what type of study is missing
- distinguish missing evidence from missing reporting discipline
- name the variables, time scale, or validation conditions that matter
- replace optimistic vagueness with conditional priorities

### Final check

- is each proposed future direction specific enough to guide research design?
- are the recommendations ranked implicitly by importance rather than listed ceremonially?

## Rebuttal or Response Letter

### Common AI-shaped signals

- every response begins with the same formula
- tone is uniformly polished but evasive
- agreement language is overused

### What must be preserved

- professionalism
- exact alignment with the reviewer comment
- accuracy about what was and was not changed

### Best revision moves

- vary response openings
- state the action taken before elaborating
- when disagreeing, do so narrowly and with evidence
- avoid praise inflation directed at reviewers

## Cover Letter

### Common AI-shaped signals

- generic relevance claims
- broad "timely and important" language
- reusable journal-submission phrasing

### What must be preserved

- the fit to the journal
- the specific contribution of the manuscript

### Best revision moves

- name the paper's angle in journal-relevant terms
- remove reusable submission clichés
- prefer fit, scope, and contribution over promotional self-description

## Cross-Section Rule

Even when polishing section by section, avoid making every revised section sound equally sharpened in the same way. A manuscript feels more human when different sections carry slightly different rhetorical pressures:

- the abstract should be controlled
- the introduction should be motivated
- the review body should be selective and technical
- hybrid design should sound like engineering judgment
- future directions should be precise and unsentimental
