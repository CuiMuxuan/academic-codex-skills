# Data Figure Body Analysis

Use this reference when writing正文 about a data figure, excluding流程图, schematics, architecture diagrams, and other non-data visuals.

## Purpose

For each data figure paragraph, state:

- what the figure is for in the paper;
- what question it answers;
- what comparison, distribution, or trend it reveals;
- what conclusion the reader should draw;
- what boundary or limitation the figure implies.

## Required Analysis Targets

When the figure is data-backed, describe at least the relevant subset of:

- best value or strongest region;
- worst value or weakest region;
- most concentrated region or densest cluster;
- sparsest region or weakest cluster;
- obvious trend, inflection, separation, or plateau;
- outlier, imbalance, or anomaly if present;
- the judgment the figure supports.

Do not list statistics mechanically. Turn the visible pattern into a manuscript claim.

## Suggested Sentence Order

1. Identify the figure's role in the argument.
2. Describe the key quantitative pattern.
3. Identify best/worst/concentrated/sparse regions when relevant.
4. State the implication for the paper's claim.
5. Note limits or exceptions if they matter.

## Allowed Wording

Use language such as:

- "Figure X shows that ..."
- "The strongest / weakest region appears in ..."
- "Values are concentrated in ..."
- "The sparsest segment occurs in ..."
- "This suggests that ..."
- "This implies that ..."

Use concrete labels from the data whenever possible. Avoid generic praise.

## Do Not

- turn the figure into a pure caption restatement;
- mention the figure without analyzing it;
- replace quantitative reading with vague approval;
- infer more than the data support;
- treat a flowchart like a data plot.

## Output Skeleton

```text
figure_type:
figure_role_in_paper:
key_quantitative_patterns:
best_or_strongest_region:
worst_or_weakest_region:
most_concentrated_region:
sparsest_region:
implication:
limits_or_exceptions:
```
