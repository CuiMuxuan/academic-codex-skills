# Academic Rhetorical Depth Modes

Use this reference when revising or drafting prose that needs a stronger academic finish without losing evidence discipline.

## 1. Mode Selection

Choose one mode before rewriting:

- `body_mechanism_mode`: use for正文、机理分析、工艺对比、结果讨论、章节主体段落.
- `summary_transition_mode`: use for摘要、引言、结论、长句过渡、段首/段末承接句.

Do not force one style onto every section. Match the mode to the section job.

## 2. Body Mechanism Mode

Use when the paragraph should sound denser, more theoretical, and more mechanism-driven.

Preferred moves:

- elevate plain descriptions into domain-level concepts when the concept is standard;
- connect micro and macro levels explicitly;
- compress repeated descriptions into one higher-level relation;
- split long lists into same-level categories;
- keep the paragraph centered on mechanism, boundary, or engineering consequence.

Allowed changes:

- replace generic wording with field-standard mechanism terms;
- increase conceptual density;
- use explicit causal or analytical connectors when they truly carry logic;
- rewrite repeated factual descriptions into a single theoretical frame.

Do not:

- invent new theory names;
- overstate causality beyond evidence;
- turn every sentence into a connector chain;
- use density to hide weak support.

## 3. Summary Transition Mode

Use when the paragraph should move fast and stay compact.

Preferred moves:

- keep the causal chain visible;
- shorten setup and removal of repeated background;
- keep only the connectors needed for real progression;
- preserve the judgment, but reduce explanatory detours;
- let each sentence push the reader to the next logical step.

Do not:

- flatten all logic into slogan-like compression;
- omit the key boundary condition;
- remove the actual conclusion just to make the prose shorter.

## 5. Style Checks

Before finalizing, check whether:

- the paragraph job is visible;
- the logic is deeper, not just smoother;
- repeated wording has been reduced or repurposed;
- the rewritten text still matches the evidence scope;
- the section mode matches the manuscript location.
