# Citation Proximity And Style Gate

Use this shared gate when drafting, revising, reviewing, auditing, polishing, or converting manuscript text that contains citations.

## Core Rule

Citations must stay close to the exact content they support:

- sentence-level claim;
- clause-level comparison;
- method, dataset, metric, parameter, or term;
- reported result;
- limitation or boundary statement;
- reviewer-response statement that depends on literature.

Do not move all citations to the final sentence of a paragraph unless the whole paragraph is a single-source summary and the target style permits that pattern.

## Multi-Claim Sentences

If one sentence contains multiple claims supported by different sources:

1. split the sentence; or
2. place each citation near the specific claim it supports.

Do not let one citation appear to support claims it does not actually cover.

## Punctuation And Style

Citation punctuation placement follows the target journal, school, or citation style.

- For bracketed numeric styles, if no target style is specified, default to placing the citation before the nearest punctuation, such as `method [3].`
- For superscript styles, follow the target guide; do not force punctuation-before placement.
- During formatting or conversion, preserve citation key positions unless the user confirms a style-driven relocation.

## Front Matter And Graphical Abstracts

摘要、highlights、摘要图默认避免正式参考文献引用；若目标期刊明确允许/要求，或摘要必须直接指向特定前人工作，则按期刊样式处理，并优先使用能独立阅读的表达方式。

## Review And Audit Checks

Flag:

- paragraph-end citation piles;
- citations far from the supported claim;
- citations attached to overly broad sentences;
- citation groups hiding disagreement or distinct evidence roles;
- citation movement caused by DOCX, Markdown, or reference-manager conversion.
